🔑 Key EDA Findings

🎂
Age is the #1 driver. Churned customers have a median age of ~45 vs ~35 for those who stayed, making it the strongest single predictor.
💸
Counter-intuitive balance effect. High-balance customers churn more. The bank is losing its most valuable customers — a critical business risk.
📅
Tenure has minimal impact. Churn distributions are nearly identical across tenure years. Customers with 0–1 year tenure show a slightly elevated risk.
🚺
Gender gap. Female customers churn at a higher rate (~25%) compared to male customers (~16%), despite similar financial profiles.
💳
Credit card ownership is neutral. Having or not having a credit card shows no significant difference in churn likelihood.
🏦
Elite attrition. High-balance multi-product customers (4 products, avg $93k) show 100% churn — the bank fails to retain its wealthiest segment.
